About

I build geospatial intelligence systems that translate land, climate, and satellite data into strategic decision-making tools. With a background in agronomy and over a decade leading technology and R&D in remote sensing, GIS, and large-scale data infrastructure, I operate at the intersection of environmental science, spatial systems, and climate strategy.

As Co-Founder and CTO of Agrosatelite Applied Geotechnology, later acquired by Serasa Experian, I led satellite monitoring products, spatial analytics pipelines, and enterprise geospatial architectures for agribusiness, financial institutions, NGOs, and public agencies.

My work contributed to initiatives such as MapBiomas and Brazil's Fourth National Communication to the UNFCCC, particularly in LULUCF emissions reporting. Today, through advisory work, I help organizations design and implement geospatial and WebGIS architectures, satellite-based land-use monitoring systems, climate and carbon intelligence frameworks, scalable spatial data infrastructure, and AI-driven environmental analytics.
